The Perfect Cover Letter Structure

A great cover letter follows a specific structure that makes it easy to read and professional.

1

Header & Contact Information

Start with your contact information and the employer's details. Match your resume header for consistency.

Example:

Your Name
Your Address | Phone | Email
[Date]
Hiring Manager Name
Company Name
Company Address

2

Salutation

Address the hiring manager by name whenever possible. Avoid generic greetings like "To Whom It May Concern."

Best Options:

3

Opening Paragraph

Start strong! Mention the position, where you saw it, and express your enthusiasm. Include 1-2 key qualifications.

Example:

"I am writing to express my enthusiastic interest in the Marketing Manager position at [Company Name], which I discovered on LinkedIn..."

4

Body Paragraphs (1-2)

Highlight your most relevant experiences and achievements. Connect your skills to the job requirements.

Strategy:

‚Ä¢ Use specific examples
‚Ä¢ Quantify achievements
‚Ä¢ Show you've researched the company

5

Closing Paragraph

Reiterate your interest, express enthusiasm for the opportunity, and include a call to action.

Example:

"I am excited about the opportunity to contribute to [Company Name]'s success and look forward to discussing my qualifications further..."

6

Closing & Signature

Use a professional closing, your signature, and typed name. Include enclosures if applicable.

Format:

Sincerely,
[Your Signature]
[Your Typed Name]
Enclosure: Resume

Dos & Don'ts of Cover Letters

Do This

  • Customize for each application
  • Address the hiring manager by name
  • Highlight relevant achievements with numbers
  • Show knowledge of the company
  • Keep it to one page maximum
  • Use professional but conversational tone
  • Proofread multiple times
  • Save as PDF with proper filename

Don't Do This

  • Send generic, one-size-fits-all letters
  • Use "To Whom It May Concern"
  • Simply repeat your resume
  • Focus only on what you want
  • Exceed one page
  • Use overly formal or casual language
  • Forget to proofread
  • Use unprofessional email addresses

Professional Tips & Strategies

7

Research the Company

Mention specific projects, values, or achievements of the company to show genuine interest.

Example:

"I was particularly impressed by [Company]'s recent initiative to [Specific Project], which aligns with my passion for [Related Interest]."

8

Address Employment Gaps

Briefly explain gaps positively. Focus on skills gained during that time.

Example:

"During my career break, I developed [New Skill] through [Activity], which enhances my ability to [Job Requirement]."

9

Keyword Optimization

Incorporate keywords from the job description naturally throughout your letter.

Strategy:

Identify 5-7 key terms from the job posting and weave them into your achievements and skills sections.

Real Examples & Templates

10

The Follow-Up Paragraph

Include a polite follow-up statement that shows initiative without being pushy.

Example:

"I will follow up on my application next week. In the meantime, please feel free to contact me at [Phone] or [Email]."

11

Email Cover Letters

For email applications, use the email body as your cover letter with a clear subject line.

Subject Line:

"Application for [Position] - [Your Name]"
"[Position] Application - [Your Name]"

12

Networking Cover Letters

When sent through a referral, mention the connection early in the letter.

Opening:

"I was referred to this opportunity by [Referrer Name], who suggested I contact you regarding the [Position] role..."
