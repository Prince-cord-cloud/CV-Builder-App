Professional Formatting Tips

1

Keep It Clean & Readable

Use clear headings, consistent fonts, and ample white space. Recruiters spend only 6-8 seconds scanning a resume, so make it easy to read.

Do:

Use 10-12pt font size, consistent bullet points, and clear section headers

2

One Page Rule (Most Cases)

For most professionals, keep your resume to one page. Senior executives with extensive experience can use two pages.

Tip:

Prioritize recent and relevant experience. Older than 10-15 years? Consider summarizing

3

Professional Contact Information

Include your name, phone, email, LinkedIn URL, and city/state. No need for full address.

Format:

John Doe | (123) 456-7890 | john.doe@email.com | linkedin.com/in/johndoe | New York, NY

Powerful Content Strategies

4

Use Action Verbs

Start bullet points with strong action verbs to demonstrate impact and initiative.

Instead of:

"Responsible for managing a team"

Use:

"Led a team of 10 to achieve 25% increase in productivity"

Tailor for Each Job

Customize your resume for every application. Match keywords from the job description.

ATS Optimization Guide

7

Simple Formatting Wins

ATS systems struggle with complex layouts. Stick to standard fonts and avoid tables, headers/footers, and columns.

Safe Fonts:

Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman, Georgia, Helvetica

8

Keyword Optimization

10

Analyze Job Descriptions

Extract keywords from job postings. Look for repeated terms and required skills.

Job Description Keywords:

Project Management, Agile, Scrum, Budget Management, Team Leadership

11

Use Synonyms

Include both specific terms and their synonyms to catch variations.

Example:

Include: Analyze, Analysis, Analytical, Data Analysis, Statistical Analysis

12

Skills Section Optimization

Organize skills by category and include both hard and soft skills.

Format:

Technical: Python, SQL, Tableau
Soft Skills: Leadership, Communication, Problem-solving

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should my resume be?

Most professionals should aim for one page. Senior executives or those with extensive relevant experience can use two pages. Recent graduates should stick to one page.

Should I include a photo on my resume?

In most countries (including the US, Canada, and UK), photos are not recommended as they can lead to unconscious bias. Exceptions include modeling, acting, or certain international markets.

How far back should work experience go?

Typically, include the last 10-15 years of relevant experience. Older experience can be summarized or omitted unless particularly relevant to the position.

What's the difference between CV and resume?

A resume is a one-page summary of skills and experience, while a CV (curriculum vitae) is a detailed document (2+ pages) used primarily in academia and research positions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

13

Spelling & Grammar Errors

One typo can cost you an interview. Use spell check, then have someone else proofread.

Tools to Use:

Grammarly, Hemingway Editor, ask a friend or mentor

14

Generic Objective Statements

Replace "Seeking a challenging position" with a powerful professional summary.

Instead:

Results-driven marketing manager with 8+ years experience increasing brand visibility...

15

Including References

Don't waste space with "References available upon request." Use that space for more achievements.

Better Use of Space:

Add another quantified achievement or relevant skill
